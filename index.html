Gynecological

Gynecological diseases refer to disorders and conditions that affect the female reproductive system, including the uterus, ovaries, fallopian tubes, vagina, and external genitalia.

Infertility: Inability to conceive after a year of regular, unprotected intercourse.
Ectopic Pregnancy: Fertilized egg implants outside the uterus, usually in a fallopian tube.

Cardiology

Cardiology is the branch of medicine that specializes in diagnosing and treating diseases and disorders of the heart and blood vessels (the cardiovascular system).

Heart Failure:A chronic condition where the heart cannot pump blood effectively to meet the body's needs.
Arrhythmias: Irregular heart rhythms, which can be too fast, too slow, or erratic.

Neurology

Neurology is the branch of medicine that deals with the diagnosis, treatment, and management of disorders affecting the nervous system. This includes the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nerves.

Central Nervous System (CNS):A chronic condition where the heart cannot pump blood effectively to meet the body's needs.
Peripheral Nervous System (PNS): Network of nerves connecting the CNS to limbs and organs, responsible for motor and sensory functions.

Hepatology

Hepatology is a branch of medicine focused on the study, diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of diseases and disorders affecting the liver, gallbladder, bile ducts, and pancreas.

Liver Biopsy:A small tissue sample is taken to diagnose cirrhosis, cancer, or inflammation.
FibroScan: Measures liver stiffness, an indicator of fibrosis or cirrhosis.

Pediatrics

Pediatrics is a branch of medicine that focuses on the health, development, and diseases of infants, children, and adolescents.

Liver Biopsy:A small tissue sample is taken to diagnose cirrhosis, cancer, or inflammation.
FibroScan: Measures liver stiffness, an indicator of fibrosis or cirrhosis.

skin diseases

Skin diseases encompass a wide range of conditions that affect the skin, hair, and nails. These conditions can be caused by infections, genetic factors, immune system dysfunction, or environmental factors.

Symptoms: Red, itchy, and inflamed skin, commonly found on the hands, feet, face, and neck.
Acne: Cause Blocked hair follicles due to excess oil production, bacteria, or hormonal changes.
